Unfortunately, part of the following article is missing and the rest is hard to read.  Here is a guesstimate of what it all says:

A year's illness from diabetes followed by paralysis caused the death of James Melvin Everett, aged 64, one of the best known farmers and hotel men in Muskingum county at the home of his son, Earl E. Everett, proprietor of the Inn hotel at 1?:?? o'clock Friday afternoon.  Mr. Everett had been in failing health for the past year and last November suffered a stroke of paralysis.  He was brought to this city and taken to the Inn hotel where he rallied from the stroke and was able to be about the city.  He suffered a second stroke early this month and since that time his condition had been critical.

Mr. Everett was born in Falls township where he lived on a farm for several years.  He later came to this city where he established the Everett hotel on South Fifth street where the Quimby theatre now stands.  He later sold his hotel and moved to a farm in Springfield township where he resided until his ??? illness. 

"Mel" Everett, as he was familiary known, was one of the most prominent farmers in this section of the country.  During his life he was the owner of several race horses and was known by race horse men through out the state.  He possessed a jovial disposition which made him friends where ever he was known.

Mr. Everett is survived by his widow and two children, Clarence Everett, well known farmer and Earl E. Everett, proprietor of the Inn Hotel, and by eight grandchildren.  The body was taken to the Dean morgue and prepared for burial.
